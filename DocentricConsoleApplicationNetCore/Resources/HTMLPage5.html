Mining Equipment / Spares

Minor spares and machine components are carried for all open cut mobile equipment at the mine sites, or by the equipment manufacturer within the region or State and would generally be available within 24 hours.  Both the excavator and loader suppliers have major depots in the Hunter Valley. Because of the large number of open cuts in the Singleton area, most manufacturers have a locally established office and stores.

Currently the haul truck fleet has sufficient capacity (92) to allow the rotation of trucks to be off site for major servicing. 

All draglines have had boom integrity checks and boom capacity upgrades in recent years and are planned on a 3-yearly cycle. All units are fitted with boom strain monitoring devices.  The draglines are being worked at 125% of the rated suspended load (RSL). This is around the maximum that Hawcroft see globally.

There are no spare booms for the draglines, a new boom would have to be constructed on site if one were to fail and this would cost up to AUD35M for the P&H 9020 and take 9 months to manufacture.

There are minimal critical spares held for the draglines, shovels, and excavators and many of these are long lead time items, i.e. up to 12 months.  This mine and HVO are the only 2 mines in the Yancoal group with draglines and only 2 of the 5 are the same (BE1370).  Komatsu (P&H) support for the 9020 dragline is less than adequate and major spares would have to be sourced from out of country.  A spare second  hand Hoist girth gear was obtained from Bengalla mine in July 2022 after a failure on site which was being fitted at the time of the 2022 survey.  a new hoist gear is on order at the time of the 2023 survey. The Marion 8200 and BE1570 draglines are different and again, access to spares may be difficult though as the 1370 is parked up and not required in the current mine plan.  Glencore have spares for these machines at other sites but as yet, no working agreement is in place to share spares.  The site relies on well managed condition monitoring programs to predict component failure.  There is no spare boom at present for the EX5500/5600 excavators and no spare handles for the shovels.  The lead time on supply of these could exceed 3 months.  

The site currently has a pair of handles for the P&H 4100XPC shovels.

Main risk to the production plan is the sensitivity of the schedule and the requirement to deliver the planned waste volumes month on month.  Significant Rainfall Events occurred across the site in 2022.

  •  Total coal mined (ROM) is 16.7Mt and total product coal is 11.2Mt.
  •  Dragline prime is forecast for 2023 at 12.1Mbcm, truck / shovel is 33.4Mbcm, excavator /truck is 48.8Mbcm, dozer push / cast blasting at 5.4Mbcm for a total of 99.7Mbcm total prime in 2023.
  •  Rehandle forecast for 2023 - Dragline is 10.0Mbcm, truck / shovel is 0.6Mbcm, excavator /truck is 1.2Mbcm for a total of 11.9Mbcm.
  •  Total Plant capacity is 12.5Mt of product coal, depending on the coal types available and some bypass is possible.
  • The North CHPP handles approximately 60% of MTW product. The South CHPP handles approximately 40% of MTW product. If product from the pits is suitable, more coal can be sent to the south plant where the 2 products can be processed.
  • Coking coal makes up approximately 15% of the total product and thermal coal makes up the remainder.
  •  The South Plant can be ramped up to handle 50% of site production if required.
  •  The operation now has two (2) pits being mined (North, and West) at any one time providing some flexibility on where coal can be drawn.
